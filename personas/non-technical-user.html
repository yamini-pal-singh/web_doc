Use Shunya without writing code

You have audio. You need a clean transcript, a translation, or a voiceover. This page walks you through the dashboard — no programming required.

What you can do from the dashboard

Upload & transcribe

Drag an MP3 or WAV in, choose a language, get a timestamped transcript.

Edit & correct

Click any word to fix errors. Speaker labels are editable too.

Export

Download as PDF, DOCX, SRT (subtitles), or plain text.

Live translate (Vāķ)

Open your mic and speak — the other side hears your words in their language.

Generate voiceover

Paste text, pick a voice and style, download an audio file.

Team sharing

Share transcripts by link, set read/edit permissions, track changes.

Using the Playground

The Shunya Labs Playground is the no-code interface for the whole stack. Upload audio, configure a few parameters, and get results instantly — no programming required.

1
Pick a workflow

Three pill-shaped tabs at the top of the page. Pick the one that matches what you're trying to do:

  • Speech to Text — transcribe an audio file (this walkthrough covers this flow).
  • Text to Speech — generate a voiceover from typed text.
  • Voice Agent — try out a real-time conversational agent.
2
Configure your job

The Configuration card has three fields. Your remaining credit balance is shown in the top-right of this card.

  • Transcription Mode — defaults to Prerecorded for files you upload.
  • Model — pick from Zero Indic, Zero Med, or Zero Codeswitch. Use Codeswitch when speakers mix English with Hindi or other Indian languages (Hinglish, Tanglish), Med for clinical recordings with drug and procedure names, and Indic for general Indian-language audio.
  • Language — leave on Auto Detect if you're not sure, or pick from the searchable list. The list is grouped (Most Used, African, etc.) so common languages are at the top.
3
Upload your audio

Click Choose Audio File in the Upload Your Audio card. Supported formats include MP3, WAV, FLAC, M4A, and more.

Once the file uploads, you'll see a green Uploaded badge with a progress bar, the filename, and the file size. The "Choose Audio File" button now shows the filename in place of its original label, and a Replace File link lets you swap it without starting over.

4
Pick features (optional)

The Features tab below the upload card lists everything you can layer on top of the basic transcript. The Code Sample tab next to it shows the equivalent API call for whatever you've selected — handy if a developer later wants to wire this up.

Features are split into two groups:

  • Audio Intelligence — Translation · Transliteration · Speaker Diarization · Speaker Identification · Word Timestamps.
  • Intelligence Features — Profanity Hashing · Custom Keyword Hashing · Intent Detection · Sentiment Analysis · Emotion Diarization · Summarisation · Keyword Normalisation.

Each option has an info icon — hover it for a one-line description of what that feature does to your transcript.

5
Run analysis

Click Run Analysis in the bottom-right of the result card. The button switches to a Running… state with a spinner, and the result area shows a Transcribing your file… indicator until processing finishes.

6
Read your results

When the job completes, the result card shows:

  • A play / scrub bar across the top — clicking jumps to a position in the audio.
  • A Detected Language pill (e.g. Detected Language: Hindi).
  • The full transcript below, including any features you turned on (timestamps, speaker labels, summary, sentiment, etc.).

Two tabs let you switch the view:

  • Transcript — human-readable, ready to copy.
  • JSON — the raw response, exactly as the API would return it.

Vāķ — live 2-way translation Illustrative

For meetings or conversations across languages:

  1. Open Vāķ from the left nav.
  2. Pick your language and the other person's language.
  3. Tap the microphone — when you speak, the other side hears a voice translation in real time. When they reply, you hear theirs.
  4. A running transcript of both sides appears in the middle for reference.
If the translation feels choppy
Move to a quieter room, and use a wired headset if you have one. Bluetooth can introduce 150–300 ms of lag that compounds with translation latency.

Generate a voiceover Illustrative

  1. Open Voices → New synthesis.
  2. Paste your script (up to ~2,000 words at a time).
  3. Pick a voice: filter by language and gender, click to preview a sample.
  4. Pick a style: Neutral for professional, Conversational for friendly, News for formal announcements, Narrative for storytelling.
  5. Adjust speed if needed (1.0 = natural, 1.3 = quick notification, 0.8 = slow/clear).
  6. Click Generate. Preview, then download as MP3 or WAV.

Common questions Illustrative

Why is there a weird word in my transcript?

Domain-specific terms (drug names, finance acronyms, brand names) sometimes get spelled out phonetically. Use Normalize key terms in the upload panel to list terms you expect, or fix them inline and save a glossary to auto-apply next time.

Why doesn't the speaker label match the person's name?

Without registered voices, Shunya labels speakers as SPEAKER_00, SPEAKER_01, etc. Rename them by clicking the label. To have Shunya name them automatically, go to Speakers → Register and upload a short clip of each person speaking alone.

How do I hide sensitive information?

In the upload panel, switch on Redact. You can:

  • Auto-detect & hash profanity
  • Mask specific phrases (e.g. "account number", "OTP") with ****

How do I share a transcript with a colleague?

Click Share in the top-right of any transcript. Pick view-only or can edit. The link is signed and expires in 30 days by default.

No dashboard yet?
If your organization deployed Shunya on-premises, your admin should have a custom dashboard URL (often voice.yourcompany.internal). Ask them for the link and credentials.
